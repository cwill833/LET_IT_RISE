Step FOUR

Your bread is now in the bulk rise process. You will start to see it growing as the yeast ferments inside your dough. This step is when the bubbles are created. If your bread, after baking, is flat and does not have bubbles it maybe because you did not let it rise long enough.

  1. WAIT
    • Your leaven needs more time to ferment.
    • Let it sit for one hour.
  1. Over Fermented
    • Your leaven has over fermented.
    • Add 25g of whole wheat flour, 25g white flour, 50g of filtered water to your starter.
    • Mix it all together using your hands.
    • Use your dough spatula to scrape off the dough on your hands into the bowl.
    • Then clean off the sides of the bowl.
    • Let it sit for one hour.
  1. READY!
    • Your leaven is ready.
    • In a large mixing bowl add 700g of warm filter water (80ÂºF)
    • Add 200g of your leaven into the bowl and stir it together.
    • Add 900g of white flour, 100g of whole wheat flour, and 20g of salt.
    • Mix everything together by hand until no bits of dry flour remain.
    • Place a damp kitchen towel over the bowl.
    • It is now time for the dough to do its first rise (fermentation). Let it sit for 30 minutes.
